Project Instructions

Design and Technology | Product Longevity

Overview

Is planned obsolescence really the best way to design products for a thriving economy? What about the impact on the environment? Billions of devices are made and sold every year and billions of them are discarded into electronic waste (e-waste) piles.


In this project you and a parter are going to explore these questions to come up with an alternative solution and then design a device that is engineered to last as long as possible. You will also research environmentally friendly ways in which your device can be discarded once it does reach the end of its life-cycle.


“There is no such thing as ‘away’. When we throw anything away it must go somewhere.” – Annie Leonard, Proponent of Sustainability

You and Your Partner will...

1) Choose a device from this list:

  • Computer (laptop or desktop)
  • Smartphone
  • Tablet
  • TV
  • Wearable Device (e.g. FitBit, Airpods, AppleWatch)
Note: if you have an idea for a different type of device and would like to research that instead - ask your teacher for permission.

2) Research the following about your chosen device:

  • What is the history behind this device and why was it invented?
  • Who uses it most? (industry or households)
  • What is it made of? (core materials)
  • What are its individual components?

3) Analyse the sustainability concern surrounding this device

Use the following questions to carry out your analysis

  • How many of these devices are made each year?
  • Is it repairable and upgradable? (how, why and why not)
  • What is its average life span?
  • How often are new models released?
  • What tends to happen to it when it is discarded?

Document your answers to ALL the questions in steps 2 and 3 and include them in your final submission

4) Design a longer lasting version of your chosen device

  • See design section below

5) Submit your research and present you design to the class

  • See showcase section below

6) Complete your project on time and submit a zip folder of your work to our Microsoft Teams channel

  • See timeline below

Design

Consider all the research you have conducted about your chosen device and use it to come up with a more sustainable design solution. Starting from the core materials, to the repairability of its components - innovate a model that will potentially end up in antique stores centuries from now. Your design must also include a disposability plan to ensure your product does not contribute to the growing e-waste piles.


"Styles come and go. Good design is a language, not a style." – Massimo Vignelli, Italian designer

Innovate Sustainability

1) Use these questions to guide your design process:

  • What core materials can be used for production? Are they abundantly available and are they recyclable?
  • How can we make the device strong and durable?
  • How many different components will it have?
  • Is it possible to repair and upgrade our device?
  • How often should new models of our device be released?
  • How can we make sure our device is disposed of in an environmentally friendly way?
  • Will people want to keep our device for a long time? (like an heirloom)

2) Create a design brief

Make a sketch of your design idea on paper or electronically. If it has internal components that are important to point out, include them in your sketch next to your device. Along with a sketch, the brief MUST include the following information:

  • Device type
  • Device name
  • List of core materials
  • List of components

3) Evaluate another team's design

In week 6, teams will review each other's designs and provide feedback. Use the feedback you receive for your product to make any necessary changes.

Upload your completed design brief to our gallery here

4) Create a Product Information Statement

Write a few paragraphs which outline the thinking behind your design. Answer the following questions for a well-rounded statement:

  • What is the product made of and why were these materials chosen?
  • How long is the predicted life-span of the product?
  • How can this product be maintained and serviced?
  • How can this product be recycled?

5) Prepare a presentation to showcase you design to the class

  • See showcase section below

Timeline

This is a full-term project and you will be given plenty of class time to work on it. You and your partner will use Microsoft Teams to collaborate via the already setup channels. Your communications must be documented and included in the final submission.

Showcase

This is your chance to sell your idea to your peers. You and your partner are going to showcase your sustainable solution and open it up for final evaluation.


Final Presentation

Prepare a 5 to 10 minute presentation showcasing your design to the class

  • Feel free to use the projector and any other ICT medium at your disposal
  • We want to see your product. Present a CAD drawing or some concept sketches/3D prints based on your design brief
  • Introduce us to your product and its longevity features
  • Tell us why you chose to design this product in particular
  • Show us how this device will be better for the environment
  • Be enthusiastic!
  • Leave time for questions from your peers at the end
