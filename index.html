Planned Obsolescence - Introduction

So what is planned obsoloescence?

Have you ever wondered why light bulbs don't last forever? Or why your phone battery doesn't stay charged for a full day anymore? Or even why some devices last longer than others even when they're technically supposed to be identical? Why is it that there are pocket watches over 100 years old that are still happily ticking away while the latest smart watch or some other wearable device from a few years ago is just about obsolete? In actual fact, the word 'obsoloescence' is derived from the word 'obsolete'...I'll let you figure out the 'planned' bit. Are you starting to see the picture?

Planned obsoloescence is exactly what it sounds like. Manufacturers engineer devices to fail at a specific time on purpose. This is not even a secret. Some argue that planned obsoloescence is actually good for the economy. Ok, I'll give them that, because more manufacturing means more jobs etc. But, is it good for the environment? This is one of the things you're going to find out through this porject. In the mean time check out this YouTube video from the crew at The Good Stuff for a deeper look into the subject.
